# Product Audit — On-site + Online Thai Lessons

Date: 2026-07-07
Product: Thai Lessons Chiang Mai

## Honest critique

The website now has strong pieces: missions, practice app, logo, and a printable workbook. But the offer still feels like several separate assets instead of one clear product system. A buyer should immediately understand:

1. What do I get if I learn online?
2. What do I get if I learn in person in Chiang Mai?
3. What happens before, during, and after the lesson?
4. Why should I pay instead of only using free missions?
5. What is the next package after the first trial?

## Main gaps

### 1. Product ladder is not explicit enough

The site has free missions, trial lessons, Starter Pack, PDFs, and practice app, but the relationship between them is not clear enough.

**Fix:** show a product ladder:
- Free: practice app + missions
- Trial: 30-minute correction session
- Starter Pack: lesson + workbook + 7-day voice correction
- On-site Walk: cafe/market/transport lesson in Chiang Mai
- 5-Lesson Survival Path: structured beginner package

### 2. On-site offer needs to feel special

In-person Chiang Mai lessons should not be only “same lesson offline.” The onsite product should feel like a local experience.

**Fix:** position on-site as:
- meet in a cafe or market
- practice with real staff/sellers when appropriate
- use printed mission cards/workbook
- teacher gives correction notes
- student receives homework voice-note task

### 3. Online offer needs a stronger remote experience

Online students need to know they still get value without being in Chiang Mai.

**Fix:** position online as:
- video-call roleplay
- screen-share mission practice
- WhatsApp voice-note correction
- replayable practice app
- downloadable workbook
- clear weekly homework

### 4. Missing “before / during / after” service design

A real service product needs process, not just content.

**Fix:** add:
- before: intake question and choose one mission
- during: phrase build, roleplay, correction
- after: voice note, homework, next lesson recommendation

### 5. Need better conversion language

Current pricing is okay, but not enough “what you get.” Buyers need deliverables and outcomes.

**Fix:** each offer should list:
- outcome
- included materials
- who it is best for
- next step CTA

## Implementation decision

Build a dedicated `/products` page that explains the complete online + on-site product system, then link it from the homepage and navigation. This makes the business feel more real and helps visitors choose the right product.
