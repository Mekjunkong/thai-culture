# Thai learning website UX/UI research

Date: 2026-07-06

This research reviews public Thai-learning websites for UX/UI patterns we can legally adapt for Thai Lessons Chiang Mai. Do not copy their lessons, images, audio, or copy verbatim. Use the design patterns and create original content.

## 1. ThaiPod101

URL: https://www.thaipod101.com/

### UX/UI strengths
- Very clear promise: learn Thai online with many short lessons.
- Strong first CTA: join/start free without a credit card.
- Audio/video-first positioning.
- Benefits are grouped into simple reasons: short lessons, study tools, teacher guidance.
- Big marketing language focused on fast progress and speaking quickly.

### What to adapt
- Lead with one clear action: start one free mission.
- Make “no credit card” and “short lessons” obvious above the fold.
- Group benefits into 3 simple cards.
- Show teacher support as a premium upgrade.

## 2. Ling Thai

URL: https://ling-app.com/learn-thai/

### UX/UI strengths
- Game-like positioning: native audio, fun games, interactive reviews.
- Clear persona targeting: travelers, expats, language enthusiasts.
- Breaks learning into small interaction types: games, reviews, real chats, momentum.
- Uses repeated CTA to start learning.

### What to adapt
- Turn lessons into small missions with game-like progress.
- Add mission cards with time, difficulty, and outcome.
- Use “momentum” elements: streak, badge, today’s win, checklist.
- Build around real chat/roleplay instead of lecture reading.

## 3. BananaThai School

URL: https://bananathaischool.com/

### UX/UI strengths
- Friendly brand personality.
- Clearly separates video courses and private lessons.
- Explains the learning journey: design your schedule, guided lessons, expert support.
- Offers free resources and live/private lesson options.

### What to adapt
- Explain our offer as a learning journey: free mission → self-study → live correction.
- Separate self-study, online lesson, and in-person Chiang Mai lesson pricing.
- Use friendly teacher/trust copy.
- Add “supported by a teacher” CTA.

## 4. Learn Thai From A White Guy

URL: https://learnthaifromawhiteguy.com/

### UX/UI strengths
- Personal founder story builds trust fast.
- Strong social proof: student count and testimonials.
- Very simple landing page hierarchy.
- Low-friction lead magnet: 5 free lessons.
- Clear problem framing: Thai feels overwhelming, this system makes it click.

### What to adapt
- Add a teacher/trust block for Mike.
- Add social-proof placeholders now; replace with real student testimonials later.
- Use “start with 5 free Thai missions” as a lead magnet.
- Keep the homepage simple and outcome-first.

## 5. Thai With Grace

URL: https://thaiwithgrace.com/

### UX/UI strengths
- Strong content ecosystem: courses, free mini-course, YouTube, ebooks, games, apps.
- Course benefits are concrete: real spoken Thai, hand-picked vocabulary, step-by-step approach.
- Uses free course and ebook lead magnets.
- Shows reviews and star ratings prominently.

### What to adapt
- Create a resource ecosystem around missions, PDF, YouTube/short videos, and live correction.
- Emphasize real spoken Thai, not textbook Thai.
- Add a free PDF/mission lead magnet near the top.
- Add course cards with clear levels and outcomes.

## Bonus reference: Learning Thai / Learn Thai Today

URL: https://learning-thai.com/

### UX/UI strengths
- No-login/no-credit-card/no-limit message is very clear.
- Book cover grid makes the product library obvious.
- Course navigation includes previous/next, collapsible lessons, and audio playback/download controls.
- Strong credibility from a real language school and printed books.

### What to adapt
- Keep free missions frictionless.
- Create a `/missions` library page.
- Add previous/next navigation inside missions later.
- Add real human audio controls and downloads later.

# Design direction for Thai Lessons Chiang Mai

## Positioning

Thai Lessons Chiang Mai should not look like a textbook course. It should feel like:

> A practical Chiang Mai Thai mission app with human correction.

## UX principles

1. **One real-life win first** — start with a usable phrase, not grammar.
2. **No-login free start** — reduce friction.
3. **Mission cards over lecture pages** — show time, level, situation, and result.
4. **Human pronunciation correction** — browser voices are only optional rough demos.
5. **Local Chiang Mai specificity** — cafes, markets, red trucks, condos, temples, immigration.
6. **Clear upgrade path** — free mission → PDF → Starter Pack → private lesson.
7. **Trust through teacher story** — explain why Mike understands expat daily life in Chiang Mai.

## Website improvements to implement

- Rework homepage hero around a free mission and no-login promise.
- Add a 3-step “how learning works” section.
- Add UX research inspired trust/feature cards.
- Add `/missions` page showing mission library.
- Add mission card design with status, time, level, outcome, and CTA.
- Add “human correction beats robot voice” message.
- Strengthen pricing section as a clear upgrade path.

## Recommended homepage flow

1. Hero: “Speak useful Thai in Chiang Mai today”
2. No-login/free trust bar
3. Mission cards
4. How it works: choose mission → practice phrase → send voice note
5. Why it is different from lecture-style courses
6. Pricing / Starter Pack
7. Teacher/trust section
