## What the site is

`learning-thai.com` is the companion-material website for the **Learn Thai Today** book/course series by Patong Language School in Phuket.

The site mainly supports people who already bought their printed books. It gives free companion materials such as audio, videos, exercises, quizzes, and lesson navigation.

## Main positioning

- Brand: **Learn Thai Today**
- Promise: easy way to learn Thai
- Strong trust signals:
  - connected to Patong Language School
  - school owner/author has lived in Thailand for decades
  - course materials used in a real language school
  - books sold through Amazon, AsiaBooks, Kinokuniya, B2S, Sengho, school shop, and other sellers
  - several products claim Amazon / AsiaBooks bestseller status
- Access model:
  - no login
  - no credit card
  - unlimited access
  - free forever for companion content

## Homepage structure

1. Header/nav
   - home
   - how to navigate
   - about
   - buy
   - contact

2. Hero / welcome message
   - thanks users for learning Thai
   - invites users to click their book cover to access materials
   - emphasizes no login, no credit card, no limit, free forever
   - asks Amazon buyers to leave a review

3. Product grid
   - clickable book covers
   - labels such as bestseller/latest edition/book 2/build wordpower/alphabet flashcards/German/Japanese editions

4. Author/school intro
   - owner introduces himself
   - explains long-term Thai learning/material development experience
   - explains materials come from a real language school and updated paperback editions
   - explains website provides audio/videos/exercises/quizzes for book owners

5. Buy prompt
   - if users do not have the books yet, they are directed to the buy page

### Read & Write Thai Today
- Beginner reading/writing course

- Includes audio for cards

### Write the Thai Alphabet Today
- Workbook companion
- Focus: listening and writing Thai letters
- Includes consonant/vowel tables, numbers, writing practice sheet downloads

### Thai Foundation
- Beginner foundation course
- Promise: easiest way to get started in Thai
- Organized like a multi-day course:
  - Getting started

## How-to / UX ideas from the site

The site explains how learners navigate course material:

- course menu changes after entering a book/course
- lessons appear in a left sidebar
- menu bar can collapse
- previous/next buttons move through lessons
- chapters can expand/collapse
- audio can be played inline
- audio menu allows download and playback speed changes
- exit course returns to course start page

## What we can learn for Thai Lessons Chiang Mai

### Good ideas to adapt legally

1. **Companion-material model**
   - Our free missions can support paid live lessons or PDF/audio products.

2. **No-login free samples**
   - Keep free missions frictionless: no signup, no credit card.

3. **Strong course-library page**
   - Create one page showing all missions/products as cards.

4. **Clear navigation inside courses**
   - Add previous/next mission buttons.
   - Add expandable mission list.
   - Add “exit mission/course” link.

5. **Audio controls**
   - But use real Thai human audio, not bad browser TTS.
   - Add slow/natural audio and downloadable MP3 later.

6. **Trust-building author section**
   - Add Mike’s local Chiang Mai story and why he teaches practical Thai to expats.

7. **Physical/digital product ecosystem**
   - Later create printable phrase cards, PDF workbook, and audio bundle.

## How our product should be different

Learning-thai.com feels like a book-companion/course library. Thai Lessons Chiang Mai should feel like a **real-life Chiang Mai mission game**.

### Our differentiation

- More local: Chiang Mai cafes, markets, songthaew/Grab, temples, condos, immigration, hospitals
- More practical: use today, not study abstract grammar first
- More interactive: choose scenario → build phrase → roleplay → mission complete
- More service-led: WhatsApp voice-note correction and live lessons
- Better for expats/digital nomads/retirees/travelers already in Chiang Mai
