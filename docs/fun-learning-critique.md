# Critique — Make Thai Lessons Chiang Mai More Fun and Easier

Date: 2026-07-06
Product: Thai Lessons Chiang Mai
Focus: Replace lecture-style studying with playful, low-pressure, practical learning.

## Honest critique

The current product is useful, but it still feels like a written course. It teaches correct information, but the learner experience is too much like reading notes in a lecture.

For expats, digital nomads, retirees, and travelers, this can feel heavy because most of them do not want to “study Thai” like school. They want to survive real moments: ordering coffee, talking to a driver, greeting a neighbor, asking a price, or solving a condo problem.

## Main problems

### 1. Too much reading before action
Current lessons start with goals, tables, explanations, and long sections. This is organized, but not emotionally fun.

**Risk:** learner opens the page, sees lots of text, and leaves before speaking.

**Better:** start each lesson with a tiny challenge:

> “You are at a cafe. Can you order iced coffee less sweet in 20 seconds?”

Then teach only what they need to win that challenge.

### 2. Tables are useful but boring
Tables help organize Thai script, romanization, and meaning. But too many tables make the lesson feel like a textbook.

**Risk:** learner memorizes passively instead of speaking.

**Better:** turn phrase tables into cards:

- Tap phrase
- Listen
- Repeat
- Pick the correct response
- Use it in a mini-scene

### 3. No game loop yet
The product has quizzes, but no “game feeling.” There is no progress path like levels, badges, streaks, unlocks, or missions.

**Risk:** learner does one lesson but does not feel pulled to continue.

**Better:** make every lesson a mission:

- Mission 1: Say hello without sounding awkward
- Mission 2: Buy mangoes at a market
- Mission 3: Order coffee less sweet
- Mission 4: Tell a driver where to stop

### 4. Not enough audio-first learning
Thai is hard to learn from text because tone, rhythm, and mouth shape matter. The page currently talks about audio-first, but Week 3 and Week 4 still do not have audio.

**Risk:** learners read romanization badly and build wrong pronunciation habits.

**Better:** every phrase should have:

- slow audio
- natural speed audio
- repeat button
- “record yourself” prompt

### 5. Missing “choose your character” personalization
Different expats need different Thai:

- cafe/food person
- market shopper
- condo renter
- motorbike/Grab user
- temple/culture visitor
- social/dating learner

**Risk:** all learners see the same sequence and some feel it is not for them.

**Better:** first screen asks: “What do you need Thai for this week?” and sends them to a path.

### 6. No visible speaking practice score
The lesson gives phrases, but learners do not see if they are improving.

**Risk:** they feel uncertain and do not book live lessons.

**Better:** add a simple confidence checklist:

- I can recognize it
- I can repeat it
- I can say it without looking
- I used it in real life

### 7. PDF is practical but not playful
The phrasebook is valuable, but still list-based. It needs more “use this today” energy.

**Better:** convert it into a challenge booklet:

- Day 1: greet 3 people
- Day 2: order 1 drink
- Day 3: ask 1 price
- Day 4: taxi/Grab phrase
- Day 5: ask for help
- Day 6: social intro
- Day 7: record your story

### 8. Online teaching room is useful for Mike, but not student-facing fun
The teaching room has lesson flow and roleplays, but could become a better interactive classroom tool.

**Better:** add:

- spin-the-situation roleplay cards
- phrase reveal cards
- “teacher says / student says” mode
- correction stickers: tone, particle, speed, confidence

## Product direction

The product should become:

> **A real-life Thai mission game for Chiang Mai expats**

Not:

> A text course about Thai language.

## New learning formula

Each lesson should follow this order:

1. **Real-life scene** — “You are at a cafe.”
2. **Tiny mission** — “Order iced coffee less sweet.”
3. **3 phrases only** — not 12 at once.
4. **Listen and repeat** — audio before reading.
5. **Choose response** — make a decision.
6. **Roleplay** — teacher or app plays the Thai person.
7. **Real-world challenge** — use it today.
8. **Book correction** — send voice note / book lesson.

## Recommended first improvement

Build one polished fun lesson first, not the whole course.

Best candidate:

## Mission 1: Order Coffee in Chiang Mai

Why:

- every expat buys drinks
- easy to understand
- immediately useful
- easy to film/market on TikTok
- good CTA to book lesson

Make it interactive:

- choose your drink
- choose sweetness
- hear Thai phrase
- repeat challenge
- mini quiz
- roleplay checkout
- “send voice note to Mike” CTA

## Success criteria

The improved product should pass these tests:

- A beginner can complete one mission in under 5 minutes.
- The first screen asks them to do something, not read something.
- Each page has more buttons/cards/audio than paragraphs.
- Every mission ends with a real-life action.
- The live lesson CTA feels like the natural next step: “Practice this with correction.”
