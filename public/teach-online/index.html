60-minute lesson flow

0–10 min · Warmup

Ask: Where do you use Thai? Food? Market? Taxi? Neighbors? Write 3 real situations for today.

10–25 min · Core phrases

Teach 6 phrases. Student listens, repeats, then says it with their own situation.

25–40 min · Roleplay

Practice one cafe/market/taxi scene. Correct gently: tone, politeness, word order.

40–50 min · Culture note

Explain when Thai people use softer language, smiling, and mai pen rai.

50–57 min · Student test

Student does the roleplay without notes. Teacher writes 3 corrections.

57–60 min · Next step

Give homework and invite them to Starter Pack or 5-lesson package.

Core teaching phrases

สวัสดีครับ / ค่ะ
sà-wàt-dii khrap / kha
Hello politely. Use khrap if male speaker, kha if female speaker.
ขอบคุณครับ / ค่ะ
khàawp-khun khrap / kha
Thank you politely.
อันนี้เท่าไหร่
an-níi thâo-rài?
How much is this?
เอาอันนี้
ao an-níi
I’ll take this one.
ไม่เผ็ดได้ไหม
mâi phèt dâai mái?
Can you make it not spicy?
พูดช้าๆ ได้ไหม
phûut cháa-cháa dâai mái?
Can you speak slowly?
ไม่เป็นไร
mâi pen rai
No problem / never mind / it’s okay. Context matters.

Roleplay cards

Cafe order

Teacher: You are cafe staff. Ask what the student wants.

Student: Order iced coffee, ask price, say thank you.

Target phrases: เอา..., เท่าไหร่, ขอบคุณครับ/ค่ะ

Market price

Teacher: You sell mangoes. Give a price and quantity.

Student: Ask price, choose one item, pay politely.

Target phrases: อันนี้เท่าไหร่, เอาอันนี้, ลดได้ไหม

Taxi / Grab pickup

Teacher: Driver calls and asks where the student is.

Student: Say location simply and ask driver to speak slowly.

Target phrases: อยู่ที่..., พูดช้าๆ ได้ไหม

Polite greeting

Teacher: You are a neighbor or shop owner.

Student: Greet, thank, and close politely.

Target phrases: สวัสดี, ขอบคุณ, ไปก่อนนะ

Homework after lesson

  2. Use one phrase in real life before next lesson and write what happened.
  3. Practice the cafe roleplay 3 times with notes, then 1 time without notes.
