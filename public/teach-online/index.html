1. Placement questions

Ask these first. Write the answers and choose the roleplay that matches their life.

  1. How long have you been in Thailand / Chiang Mai?
  2. Where do you need Thai most: food, market, transport, condo, dating, work, temple, immigration?
  3. Can you read Thai script? If not, use romanization only today.
  4. What Thai phrase do you already know?
  5. What real situation do you want to handle before our next lesson?

2. 60-minute lesson flow

0–8 min · Warmup

Ask placement questions. Choose one target situation: cafe, market, driver, condo, or greeting.

8–22 min · Core phrases

Teach 6 phrases. Student listens, repeats slowly, then says each phrase with khrap/kha.

22–35 min · Pronunciation

Correct only 2 things: final sound and rhythm. Do not overwhelm with all tones.

35–48 min · Roleplay

Run the real situation 3 times: with notes, half notes, no notes.

48–55 min · Culture note

Explain why the phrase feels polite, direct, too casual, or safe.

55–60 min · Homework

Give one voice-note task and invite the student to Starter Pack or 5-lesson package.

4. Roleplay cards

Cafe order

Teacher: You are cafe staff. Ask what the student wants and sweetness level.

Student: Order iced coffee, less sweet, one glass, and ask price.

Target: เอากาแฟเย็นหนึ่งแก้ว หวานน้อยครับ/ค่ะ

Market price

Teacher: You sell mangoes. Give a price and quantity.

Student: Ask price, buy one item, ask for a bag.

Target: อันนี้เท่าไหร่ / เอาหนึ่งอัน / มีถุงไหม

Driver pickup

Teacher: Driver calls and asks where the student is.

Student: Say location simply and ask driver to stop here.

Target: อยู่ตรงนี้ / จอดตรงนี้ / ขอบคุณครับ

Condo problem

Teacher: You are condo staff.

Student: Explain there is a problem and ask for technician.

Target: มีปัญหา / เสียแล้ว / เรียกช่างได้ไหม
