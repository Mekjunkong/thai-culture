# Week 4: Transport, Temples, Markets & Local Etiquette

## Goal
By the end of this lesson you can move around Chiang Mai more comfortably: talk to drivers, behave politely at temples and markets, ask for help, and understand common etiquette mistakes foreigners make.

## 1. Transport and directions

| Situation | Thai script | Romanization | Meaning |
|---|---|---|---|
| Can you go here? | ไปที่นี่ได้ไหมครับ / คะ | bpai thîi-nîi dâai mái khrap/kha? | Can you go here? |
| I am here | ผม/ฉันอยู่ตรงนี้ | phǒm/chǎn yùu dtrong-níi | I am here. |
| Stop here | จอดตรงนี้ครับ / ค่ะ | jàwt dtrong-níi khrap/kha | Stop here. |
| Turn left | เลี้ยวซ้าย | líao sáai | Turn left. |
| Turn right | เลี้ยวขวา | líao khwǎa | Turn right. |
| Go straight | ตรงไป | dtrong bpai | Go straight. |
| Is it far? | ไกลไหมครับ / คะ | glai mái khrap/kha? | Is it far? |
| Is it near? | ใกล้ไหมครับ / คะ | glâi mái khrap/kha? | Is it near? |

## 3. Temple etiquette language

Useful words:

| Thai | Romanization | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| วัด | wát | temple |
| พระ | phrá | monk / Buddha image context |
| รองเท้า | raawng-tháao | shoes |
| ถ่ายรูปได้ไหม | thàai-rûup dâai mái? | Can I take a photo? |
| เข้าได้ไหม | khâo dâai mái? | Can I enter? |

Temple behavior basics:

- remove shoes when signs or local behavior shows it
- dress modestly: shoulders and knees covered when needed
- do not point feet at Buddha images or monks
- speak softer than normal
- ask before taking photos in sensitive areas

## 4. Mini dialogue: Grab or red truck pickup

**Driver:** อยู่ไหนครับ  
*yùu nǎi khrap?*  
Where are you?

**Student:** ผมอยู่ตรงนี้ครับ ใกล้ประตูครับ  
*phǒm yùu dtrong-níi khrap, glâi bprà-dtuu khrap*  
I am here, near the gate.

**Driver:** โอเคครับ  
*oo-khee khrap*  
Okay.

**Student:** จอดตรงนี้ครับ ขอบคุณครับ  
*jàwt dtrong-níi khrap, khàawp-khun khrap*  
Stop here, thank you.

## 5. Cultural shortcuts for expats

| If you feel... | Thai strategy |
|---|---|
| confused | smile + say **ไม่เข้าใจครับ/ค่ะ** |
| someone helps you | say **ขอบคุณมากครับ/ค่ะ** |
| a situation is tense | lower your voice, slow down, use **ขอโทษครับ/ค่ะ** |
| you are unsure about temple behavior | watch locals first, then copy politely |

## 6. Graduation roleplay

The student must complete three scenes without notes:

1. greet a neighbor or shop owner
2. buy one item and ask the price
3. tell a driver where to stop

Teacher gives feedback in three categories:

- pronunciation
- politeness
- confidence / speed

Use Thai in one transport situation. If that feels too hard, record a voice note pretending to talk to a driver:
